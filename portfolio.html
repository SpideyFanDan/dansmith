Learning Design Portfolio

Workplace-based problems

Assessment activity for skills-based learning

This video shows a sample of the introduction to a workplace-based problem developed for a software engineering course. The problem is designed to be used as an assessment activity for students to demonstrate their skills in a real-world scenario. There was a drag and drop feature created to mimic the use of Kanban boards in the workplace. The problem was developed using Articulate Storyline 360, but for proprietary reasons, I have only shared the design features and ideas surrounding its development.

High Level Design

Sample high level design skeleton

In this video, I present the high level design for a software engineering immersive course. I discuss the design thinking that went into it, particularly in relation to the Kirkpatrick model and incorporating real-world scenarios. The course was transformed from instructor-led training to a blended learning environment, catering to non-tech individuals seeking to reskill in software development. I provide an overview of the course structure, including different tracks, assessments, and projects. The video also highlights the importance of assessments in determining the appropriate track for students.

Modularization

Sample modularization framework

In this video, I showcase a modularization example that I created for a software engineering immersive program. I explain how I structured the content and developed a competency and skills map to create e-learning modules and develop assessments for the updated version of the course. I also discuss the importance of categorizing the content with conceptual vs technical skills to provide insights into managing the content within an LMS and break down the content into micro learnings.
